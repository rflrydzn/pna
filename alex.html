Russiaâ€™s gasoline output rises by 3.3%, diesel by 6.4%

September 28, 2023, 6:08 pm


MOSCOW - Gasoline production in Russia has seen a 3.3 percent increase year-on-year from January to August 2023, and diesel fuel has risen by 6.4 percent, according to Russian Deputy Prime Minister Alexander Novak. He shared this information during a meeting with Russian President Vladimir Putin, where he also discussed government proposals to ensure a stable long-term domestic supply of oil products. The government is considering various measures, including adjusting damper parameters and potentially raising the protective duty on oil product exports from 20,000 rubles to 50,000 rubles per ton for resellers. Novak believes it might be possible to entirely ban exports of oil products obtained in the domestic market to ensure stability.

Novak highlighted that the recent increase in gasoline stock prices in the last two days is a correction following a significant recent decline. After a notable drop, there has been a 1-2 percent correction in the past two days. He emphasized that this correction is normal, as current stock exchange levels accurately reflect the true situation in the market. The government aims to make decisions on systemic measures in the near future to have a long-term impact on stabilizing the domestic market situation.
