Healthy profit-taking at local bourse; peso ends Q3 strong

By Kris Crismundo

September 29, 2023, 8:13 pm


MANILA - The local stock market saw a drop on the final day of the third quarter due to "healthy profit-taking." The Philippine Stock Exchange index (PSEi) ended its six-day winning streak, falling by 64.28 points to close at 6,321.24. Despite this, it had gained for the second straight week, up 2.9 percent over four of the last five weeks. Among the sectors, only the Mining and Oil counter showed growth, while the Industrial sector saw the biggest decline. Portfolio managers are closely watching bond movements and economic indicators.

In currency news, the Philippine peso closed Q3 2023 stronger, finishing Friday's trading at 56.58 to a US dollar. It opened the day at 56.75 and maintained strength throughout the trading session. The peso's performance was influenced by a downward correction in the US dollar against major global currencies and a recent dip in global crude oil prices. Trade volume also increased for the day, indicating potential stability in the peso exchange rate for the coming week, with a projected range of 56.30 to 56.80 levels.
